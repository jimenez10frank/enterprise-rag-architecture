# 01 — Vectors and Embeddings

## What it is

An embedding model reads a piece of text and outputs a list of floating-point numbers — the vector. Two pieces of text that mean the same thing end up with similar vectors even if they use different words. The similarity between two vectors is measured by cosine similarity: 1.0 means identical meaning, 0.0 means unrelated.

## Why it matters for this project

Our corpus is Dutch legal text. A user asks "mag ik mijn werkruimte aftrekken?" and the relevant chunk says "De aftrekbaarheid van een werkruimte aan huis is geregeld in artikel 3.16 Wet IB 2001." Those two strings share almost no words, but a good multilingual embedding model maps them close together. Without embeddings, keyword search would miss this entirely.

The other reason it matters: the vector is what we store in Qdrant. Every chunk in the database is represented as a 3072-dimensional vector alongside its metadata. When a query comes in, we embed the query and find the nearest vectors. That's the entire retrieval mechanism.

## Key parameters and decisions

- **Model (demo):** `text-embedding-3-large` — 3072 dimensions, OpenAI API, strong Dutch performance.
- **Model (production):** `BAAI/bge-m3` — self-hosted, multilingual, MIT licensed, single GPU. Non-negotiable for classified documents because sending classified text to OpenAI's API is a GDPR/data residency violation.
- **Dimension:** 3072. Higher dimensions generally mean better recall. The tradeoff is memory. At 20M chunks × 3072 dims × 4 bytes = ~240 GB raw — which is why we quantize.
- **Distance metric:** Cosine similarity. We normalize vectors on ingestion so cosine and dot product are equivalent. Qdrant config uses `"distance": "Cosine"`.
- **Important:** OpenAI-generated embeddings and bge-m3-generated embeddings live in **separate Qdrant collections**. Their vector spaces are not comparable — mixing them would produce garbage results.

## Concrete example

Query: "thuiswerkkosten zzp'er 2024"
Chunk: "Artikel 3.17 Wet IB 2001 — Aftrekbaarheid werkruimte voor IB-ondernemer"

After embedding both, cosine similarity ≈ 0.78. That's high enough to surface in top-50 initial retrieval. After reranking it might rise to top-3.

A query about "BTW tarief aardappelen" would score ~0.12 against that same chunk — too different to appear.
